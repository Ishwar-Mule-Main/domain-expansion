## 1. Executive Summary

Domain Expansion is a 100% remote, full-service digital agency headquartered in Latur/Solapur, Maharashtra, India. Founded in 2024 by Ishwar B. Mule, the agency delivers four integrated pillars of service — Marketing Expansion, Development Expansion, Design Expansion, and AI Expansion — to clients across India, the US, UK, and Southeast Asia.

This PRD defines the complete product requirements for two interconnected digital products:

**Product 1 — The Domain Expansion Agency Website** (`domainexpansion.in`): A high-performance, dark-premium, animated marketing and portfolio site that establishes the brand's credibility, showcases proven client results across 21 documented projects, generates qualified inbound leads (target: 50+/month), and itself functions as a living case study of the agency's own design and technical capabilities.

**Product 2 — TechGuild** (`techguild.domainexpansion.in` evolving to `techguild.in`): A B2B SaaS marketplace exclusively connecting verified digital agencies with serious business clients, fundamentally different from Upwork or Freelancer by eliminating individual freelancers entirely and operating as a curated, agency-only platform. TechGuild is positioned as the premier agency marketplace in South Asia.

Both products share a single codebase, design system, and backend infrastructure. The agency website generates leads and builds credibility today; TechGuild represents the company's long-term SaaS revenue play.

## 4. Target Audience & User Personas

### Persona 1 — The Indian SME Business Owner (Primary)
**Who:** Owner/co-founder of a small-to-medium business in India (food & beverage, real estate, retail, manufacturing, hospitality). Annual revenue ₹1 Cr – ₹20 Cr.
**Demographics:** Age 30-50, located in Tier 1/2 Indian cities. Decision-maker with limited digital marketing knowledge.
**Core Needs:** Affordable, reliable digital marketing. Social media presence. A professional website. Lead generation.
**Pain Points:** Has been burned by freelancers who promised results but delivered nothing measurable. Doesn't understand what to look for in an agency. Worried about wasting money. Wants proof before committing.
**Digital Behavior:** Searches Google for agencies. Checks Instagram portfolios and Google Reviews. Gets referrals from other business owners. WhatsApp is the preferred communication channel.
**Goals on the Website:** See real portfolio work from similar industries. Understand what services are available and approximate pricing. Contact via WhatsApp for a quick conversation.
**Success Trigger:** A portfolio card showing a client from a similar industry with a specific result metric (e.g., "230% lead increase for Meat Me Foods — same industry, same city size").

### Persona 2 — The International Startup Founder (High Value)
**Who:** Early-stage founder in US/UK/UAE/Singapore needing SEO, growth marketing, React/Next.js development, or AI integration. Budget-conscious but seeking proven execution capability.
**Demographics:** Age 25-40, technical background or surrounded by technical advisors. Evaluating Indian agencies for cost arbitrage without quality compromise.
**Core Needs:** SEO to build organic traffic, performance marketing for user acquisition, React/Next.js frontend development, AI-powered product features.
**Pain Points:** US/UK agency rates are 5-10x higher. Fears communication issues and timezone misalignment. Needs to trust the agency's technical credibility before engaging.
**Digital Behavior:** Searches "affordable digital agency India" or "React development agency India." Checks Clutch, LinkedIn, GitHub profiles, and detailed case studies with specific traffic/ranking numbers.
**Goals on the Website:** Read multiple case studies with verifiable metrics. Understand the exact process for each service. Book a consultation call directly.
**Success Trigger:** A case study showing specific keyword rankings, traffic growth percentages, and a testimonial from an international client.

### Persona 3 — The Corporate Marketing Manager (Retainer Opportunity)
**Who:** In-house marketing lead at a mid-size company needing external execution capacity. Manages a team but lacks specialists in SEO, paid ads, advanced design, or AI.
**Core Needs:** Reliable, communication-consistent agency for ongoing campaign execution. Social media management, paid advertising, email marketing, UI/UX design.
**Pain Points:** Agency reliability and timeline adherence. Communication breakdowns. Reporting quality. Wants a single point of contact.
**Goals on the Website:** Review the full service list in detail. See specific client work from comparable company sizes. Submit a detailed RFQ.
**Success Trigger:** Detailed service pages with process methodology, specific tools used, and measurable outcomes. A professional contact form with service-specific fields.

### Persona 4 — The TechGuild Agency Owner (Platform User)
**Who:** Founder of another small digital agency (2-15 people) struggling with consistent lead generation. Spending significant budget on ads and cold outreach with low ROI.
**Core Needs:** Reliable pipeline of pre-qualified client projects. Platform to showcase verified agency credentials. Reduction in lead acquisition cost.
**Pain Points:** Lead generation is expensive and unpredictable. Competing with large agencies on generalist platforms. Client trust is hard to establish without a verified review history.
**Goals on TechGuild:** Register agency profile. Browse available client projects that match specialization. Submit competitive proposals. Build a review-backed reputation.

### Persona 5 — The TechGuild Client (Platform User)
**Who:** Business owner or project manager who has tried hiring on Freelancer/Upwork and been disappointed by individuals. Needs a team with reliable delivery and accountability.
**Core Needs:** Access to vetted, verified agencies. Ability to compare proposals from multiple agencies. Confidence that the agency will deliver what they promise.
**Goals on TechGuild:** Post a project brief clearly. Receive structured agency proposals. Compare agencies by specialization, reviews, and portfolio. Hire with confidence.

## 5. Complete Service Structure & Taxonomy

The full-spectrum digital marketing pillar covering every channel through which a business can reach, acquire, and retain customers online.

- Social Media Marketing & Management — Content strategy, daily/weekly post creation, community management, growth campaigns, and influencer coordination across Instagram, LinkedIn, Facebook, Twitter/X, and YouTube.

- Content Marketing & Strategy — Blog strategy, SEO-optimized article writing, content calendar management, pillar page creation, and long-form content production.

End-to-end software and web development, from simple marketing websites to complex SaaS platforms and custom enterprise software.

Sub-services:

- Web App Development — React and Next.js frontend, Node.js/Python backend, REST and GraphQL APIs. SPA, SSR, and SSG architectures.
- Mobile App Development — React Native (cross-platform iOS/Android), Flutter (Google's cross-platform framework). End-to-end from design to App Store/Play Store submission.
- Custom Software Development — Bespoke business applications, internal tools, and operational workflow software.
- API Development & Integration — Third-party API integration (CRMs, payment gateways, marketing tools), custom REST API development, webhook implementations.

- SaaS Product Development — Full-cycle SaaS development including multi-tenancy architecture, subscription billing, user management, and admin dashboards.

Comprehensive visual and UX design services that bridge brand strategy and digital execution.

Cutting-edge artificial intelligence services that automate operations, personalize marketing, and build AI-powered products.

Sub-services:

### 6.12 TechGuild Landing Page (`/techguild`)

**Strategic Purpose:** Introduce TechGuild as a distinct product, collect waitlist signups from both agencies and clients, build anticipation for platform launch (Q3 2026).

**TechGuild Product Concept:**
TechGuild is a verified B2B agency marketplace. Unlike Upwork, Freelancer, or Fiverr — which list individuals and are plagued by quality unpredictability — TechGuild lists ONLY verified digital agencies. Clients post project briefs; agencies respond with structured proposals. The platform handles matching, communication, milestone tracking, and (Phase 3) escrow payments.

Key differentiators vs. Upwork/Freelancer: agency-only (no individuals), manual verification of agencies before listing, structured proposal process, milestone-based project tracking, built-in review system weighted toward outcome quality.

**Section 1 — Hero**
"TechGuild: Where Great Agencies Meet Great Clients." "Coming Q3 2026" chip in orange. Subheadline: "The first B2B marketplace built exclusively for verified digital agencies. No freelancers. Tabbed section with two views:

Hire the right fit.

For Agencies (3 steps):

Step 2: Browse & Propose — Get access to a curated feed of active client projects matching your specializations. Submit proposals with your pricing and timeline. Step 3: Win More Clients — Build your TechGuild review profile. Turn great work into a self-reinforcing lead pipeline.
